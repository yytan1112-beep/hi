Our Story

Three generations, one pot

My grandfather sold bak kut teh from a pushcart near the old Klang wet market, ladling broth into tins for dock workers heading to their morning shift. He never wrote the recipe down — it lived in his hands, in how dark the soy should go and how long the herbs should sit.

We still cook it his way: whole garlic bulbs, a hand-tied herbal packet, and ribs that simmer until the bone gives without asking. No shortcuts, no MSG, no rushing a pot that wants six hours.
